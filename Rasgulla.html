Rasgulla

Rasgulla, a culinary marvel hailing from the eastern regions of India, emerges as a soft and spongy poetry in sugar syrup. Crafted from chhena (freshly made cheese), these delicate orbs epitomize the art of achieving the perfect balance between sweetness and ethereal softness. Served chilled, each bite is a refreshing journey into the heart of sweetness, leaving an indelible mark on the palate.

Ingredients

  1. 1 liter milk
  2. 1/4 cup lemon juice
  3. 1 cup sugar
  4. 4 cups water

Directions

  1. Boil milk and add lemon juice to curdle it. Strain the chenna (curdled milk).
  2. Knead the chenna into a smooth dough.
  3. Shape the dough into small balls.
  4. Boil sugar and water to make a syrup. Add the chenna balls to the syrup.
  5. Cook covered for 15-20 minutes until the rasgullas are spongy and doubled in size.

Adjust the size of the rasgulla balls according to your preference. The key is to knead the chenna well to achieve a smooth texture for the rasgullas.
