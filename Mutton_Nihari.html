Mutton Nihari

Mutton Nihari, a culinary gem that originated in the royal kitchens of the Mughals, is a testament to the art of slow-cooked perfection. This dish, named after the Arabic word "Nihar," meaning daybreak, reflects its traditional preparation overnight. Meticulously slow-cooked until the meat reaches an unparalleled level of tenderness, Mutton Nihari is a melange of flavors heightened by a secret blend of spices. The resulting aromatic broth is a medley of saffron, cardamom, and other spices, creating an immersive experience that transcends the ordinary. Served with naan or steamed rice, Mutton Nihari beckons with its comforting aroma and unrivaled richness.  

    

Ingredients

  1. Mutton - 500g
  2. Wheat flour - 2 tbsp
  3. Ghee - 4 tbsp
  4. Onions - 2, finely sliced
  5. Ginger-garlic paste - 2 tbsp
  6. Red chili powder - 1 tbsp
  7. Turmeric powder - 1/2 tsp
  8. Coriander powder - 1 tbsp
  9. Whole spice mix (cinnamon, cardamom, cloves) - 1 tbsp
  10. Salt - to taste
  11. Water - 4 cups
  12. Coriander leaves - for garnish
  13. Ginger slices - for garnish
  14. Green chilies - for garnish

Directions

  1. Heat ghee in a pot and sauté sliced onions until golden brown.
  2. Add mutton and ginger-garlic paste, cook until meat changes color.
  3. Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, and the whole spice mix. Mix well.
  4. Stir in wheat flour and cook for a few minutes until it releases a nutty aroma.
  5. Pour in water and bring it to a boil. Then, reduce heat and simmer until mutton is tender.
  6. Adjust salt to taste and garnish with coriander leaves, ginger slices, and green chilies.

Enjoy your flavorful and hearty Mutton Nihari! Adjust the spice levels and thickness according to your taste preferences.
