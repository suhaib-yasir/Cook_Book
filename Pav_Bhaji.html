Pav Bhaji

Pav Bhaji, a street food sensation and comfort food extraordinaire, emanates a tantalizing aroma that beckons all who encounter it. This beloved dish hails from the bustling streets of Mumbai and has transcended regional borders to become a culinary treasure. At its heart is a medley of mashed vegetables, simmered to perfection in a spiced tomato-based gravy. The symphony of flavors is elevated by a generous dollop of butter, creating a rich and luscious consistency. Served alongside buttered pav (soft bread rolls), Pav Bhaji is not merely a dish; it's an experience that warms the soul and captures the essence of communal delight. The vibrant colors, robust flavors, and the communal act of savoring this dish make Pav Bhaji an emblematic representation of the vivacity and warmth embedded in Indian street food culture.

Ingredients

  1. 3 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  2. 1 cup cauliflower, finely chopped
  3. 1/2 cup green peas, boiled
  4. 1/2 cup carrots, finely chopped
  5. 1/2 cup bell peppers (capsicum), finely chopped
  6. 1 cup tomatoes, finely chopped
  7. 1 large onion, finely chopped
  8. 1/4 cup green beans, finely chopped
  9. 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  10. 2 tablespoons pav bhaji masala
  11. 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  12. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  13. Salt to taste
  14. 1/4 cup butter
  15. Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  16. Lemon wedges for serving
  17. Pav (dinner rolls)
  18. Butter for toasting

Directions

  1. In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of butter.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
  5. Add chopped cauliflower, green peas, carrots, bell peppers, and green beans. Mix well.
  6. Add pav bhaji masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix the spices with the vegetables.
  7. Once the vegetables are cooked, mash them using a potato masher or the back of a ladle.
  8. Add boiled and mashed potatoes. Mix well and continue mashing.
  9. Add the remaining butter and mix it into the bhaji. Allow it to simmer for a few more minutes.
  10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  11. Cut the pav (dinner rolls) into halves.
  12. Heat a pan, add a little butter, and toast the pav until they are lightly browned and buttered.
  13. Serve the hot bhaji with the buttered pav.
  14. Garnish with more butter on top, if desired.
  15. Serve with lemon wedges on the side.

Enjoy your delicious Pav Bhaji! It's a flavorful and satisfying dish that is loved by people of all ages. Adjust the spice levels and butter quantity according to your taste preferences.
