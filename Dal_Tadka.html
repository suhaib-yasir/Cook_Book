Dal Tadka

Dal Tadka is a quintessential Indian dish that features lentils tempered with aromatic spices, giving it a distinctive and flavorful profile. The dish typically involves cooking yellow lentils (often masoor dal or toor dal) until soft and then adding a tempering, known as "tadka" in Hindi. The tadka is created by heating ghee or oil and adding mustard seeds, cumin seeds, asafoetida (hing), garlic, ginger, and sometimes green chilies.

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup yellow split lentils (toor dal)
  2. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  3. 1 teaspoon salt
  4. 3 cups water
  5. 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter) or oil
  6. 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  7. 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  8. 2-3 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  9. 1 inch ginger, finely chopped
  10. 2 green chilies, chopped (adjust to taste)
  11. 1 medium-sized onion, finely chopped
  12. 2 medium-sized tomatoes, finely chopped
  13. 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  14. 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  15. Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Directions

  1. Rinse the toor dal under cold water until the water runs clear.
  2. In a pressure cooker, combine the dal, turmeric powder, salt, and water.
  3. Pressure cook for about 3-4 whistles or until the dal is soft and well-cooked. If you don't have a pressure cooker, you can cook the dal in a regular pot until it becomes soft.
  4. In a separate pan, heat ghee or oil.
  5. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds. Allow them to splutter.
  6. Add finely chopped garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Saut√© until the raw smell disappears and the garlic turns golden brown.
  7. Add finely chopped onions and cook until they become translucent.
  8. Add finely chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate.
  9. Add red chili powder and garam masala. Mix well.
  10. Once the dal is cooked, mash it with the back of a spoon or a ladle to achieve a smooth consistency.
  11. Pour the tadka (tempering) into the dal and mix well. Adjust the consistency by adding water if needed.
  12. Simmer the dal for a few minutes to allow the flavors to blend.
  13. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  14. Serve hot with rice or Indian bread like roti or naan.

Enjoy your delicious Dal Tadka! Adjust the spice levels according to your taste.
