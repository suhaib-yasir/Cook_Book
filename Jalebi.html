Jalebi

Imagine the captivating swirls of Jalebi, like golden comets in a sugary galaxy. These deep-fried spirals, soaked in saffron-infused sugar syrup, are a feast for the eyes and the palate. The symphony of textures, from the initial crunch to the syrupy succulence, transforms Jalebi into an edible art installation. It's not just a sweet; it's a spectacle that invites you to savor the joy of indulgence.  

    

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  2. 1 tablespoon gram flour (besan)
  3. 1 cup yogurt (curd)
  4. 1 cup sugar
  5. 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  6. 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder (for color)
  7. 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  8. A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  9. Oil or ghee for frying

Directions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, gram flour, yogurt, and baking soda. Mix well to form a smooth batter. Let it ferment for a few hours or overnight.
  2. Add sugar to a saucepan with enough water to make a syrup. Heat until the sugar dissolves, and the syrup reaches a one-string consistency. Add cardamom powder and saffron strands, and then set aside.
  3. Transfer the fermented batter to a squeeze bottle or a piping bag with a small hole.
  4. Heat oil or ghee in a pan for deep frying.
  5. Squeeze the batter in a spiral or pretzel shape directly into the hot oil. Fry until the jalebis are golden brown and crispy.
  6. Remove the fried jalebis and immediately dip them into the prepared sugar syrup, ensuring they are well-coated.
  7. Let the jalebis soak in the syrup for a few seconds, then remove and serve hot.

Enjoy the sweet and delightful Jalebis! They are best enjoyed fresh and warm. Adjust the sugar syrup thickness according to your preference.
