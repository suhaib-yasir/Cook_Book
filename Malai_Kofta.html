Malai Kofta

Malai Kofta, a regal gem in the crown of Indian cuisine, beckons with its opulence and indulgence. This dish is a triumph of culinary artistry, featuring luscious koftas (dumplings) made from paneer and potatoes, bathed in a velvety tomato and cream-based curry. The koftas, adorned with a symphony of dry fruits, impart a luxurious texture and a burst of richness with every bite. Malai Kofta transcends the ordinary, presenting a gastronomic experience that seamlessly combines elegance, flavor, and tradition, making it a perennial favorite in the realm of Indian culinary delights.

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup grated paneer (Indian cottage cheese)
  2. 2 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  3. 2 tablespoons cornflour or besan (gram flour)
  4. Salt to taste
  5. 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  6. 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  7. Oil for frying
  8. 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  9. 1 large onion, finely chopped
  10. 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  11. 1 cup tomato puree
  12. 1/2 cup cashew nuts, soaked in warm water
  13. 1/4 cup fresh cream or malai
  14. 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  15. 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  16. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  17. 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  18. Salt to taste
  19. 1 cup water (adjust as needed)
  20. Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Directions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine grated paneer, mashed potatoes, cornflour or besan, salt, red chili powder, and garam masala.
  2. Mix well to form a smooth and pliable dough.
  3. Divide the mixture into small portions and shape them into round or oval koftas.
  4. Heat oil in a pan for deep frying.
  5. Fry the shaped koftas until they turn golden brown. Remove and place them on paper towels to absorb excess oil.
  6. Grind soaked cashew nuts into a smooth paste by adding a little water.
  7. Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  8. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute until the raw smell disappears.
  9. Add tomato puree and cook until the oil starts to separate from the masala.
  10. Add red chili powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.
  11. Stir in the cashew paste and fresh cream. Mix until well combined.
  12. Add water to achieve the desired consistency of the curry. Bring it to a gentle simmer.
  13. Just before serving, add the fried koftas to the simmering curry.
  14. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  15. Serve Malai Kofta hot with naan, roti, or rice.

Enjoy your delicious Malai Kofta! It's a rich and indulgent dish that is sure to be a hit at any special occasion. Adjust the spice levels according to your taste preferences.
