Rajma

Rajma, an embodiment of comfort in North Indian cuisine, beckons with its heartwarming allure. This quintessential dish features red kidney beans luxuriating in a thick, spiced tomato-based gravy. Slow-cooked to perfection, the velvety texture of the beans intertwines with a medley of aromatic spices, creating a robust flavor profile that is both nostalgic and deeply satisfying. Served generously over steamed rice, Rajma transcends its humble ingredients, becoming a culinary embrace that resonates with warmth and tradition. Each spoonful tells a story of familial kitchens, shared meals, and the enduring legacy of this beloved dish in Indian households. Rajma is not just a meal; it's a comforting journey to the heart of North Indian culinary heritage.

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup dried rajma (red kidney beans), soaked overnight
  2. 3 cups water
  3. 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  4. 1 large onion, finely chopped
  5. 1 large tomato, finely chopped
  6. 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  7. 1 green chili, chopped (adjust to taste)
  8. 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  9. 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  10. 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  11. 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  12. 1 teaspoon garam masala
  13. Salt to taste
  14. Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Directions

  1. Rinse the soaked rajma under cold water.
  2. In a pressure cooker, combine rajma and water. Cook until the rajma is soft and easily mashed. This usually takes about 15-20 minutes.
  3. In a separate pan, heat oil or ghee.
  4. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  5. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  6. Add ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chili. Sauté for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  7. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate.
  8. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.
  9. Once the rajma is cooked, mash a portion of the beans with the back of a spoon or a ladle to thicken the curry.
  10. Pour the tadka (tempering) into the rajma and mix well.
  11. Simmer the rajma for a few minutes to let the flavors meld. Adjust the consistency by adding more water if needed.
  12. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  13. Serve hot with rice or Indian bread like roti or naan.

Enjoy your flavorful Rajma! Adjust the spice levels according to your taste. This dish is often served with rice and is a popular comfort food in many parts of India.
