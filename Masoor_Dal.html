Masoor Dal

Masoor Dal, a soul-warming creation, beckons with the comforting embrace of red lentils. This humble yet hearty dish epitomizes simplicity in Indian cuisine, where red lentils are meticulously cooked to tender perfection. Infused with a medley of aromatic spices, the dal emanates a fragrant allure that entices both the senses and the spirit. Its wholesome nature and quick preparation make it a go-to choice for nourishment, offering a bowl of solace and sustenance in every spoonful. Masoor Dal embodies the essence of home-cooked warmth, reminding us that culinary elegance can often be found in the most straightforward and nourishing of dishes.

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup red lentils (masoor dal), washed and soaked for 30 minutes
  2. 3 cups water
  3. 1 medium-sized onion, finely chopped
  4. 1 large tomato, finely chopped
  5. 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  6. 1 green chili, chopped (adjust to taste)
  7. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  8. 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  9. 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  10. 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  11. Salt to taste
  12. 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  13. 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  14. A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  15. Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  16. Lemon wedges for serving (optional)

Directions

  1. In a saucepan, combine soaked red lentils and water. Bring to a boil.
  2. Skim off any foam that rises to the surface.
  3. Add turmeric powder and simmer until the lentils are soft and cooked through. This usually takes about 15-20 minutes.
  4. In a separate pan, heat oil or ghee.
  5. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  6. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  7. Add ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chili. Sauté for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  8. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate.
  9. Add red chili powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  10. Once the lentils are cooked, mash them with the back of a spoon or a ladle to achieve a smooth consistency.
  11. Pour the tadka (tempering) into the lentils and mix well.
  12. In a small pan, heat a bit more oil or ghee.
  13. Add a pinch of asafoetida (hing) and pour this tempering over the lentils. This step is optional but adds a unique flavor.
  14. Simmer the dal for a few minutes to let the flavors meld. Adjust the consistency by adding more water if needed.
  15. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  16. Serve hot with rice or Indian bread like roti or naan.
  17. Serve with lemon wedges on the side for a tangy kick. (optional)

Enjoy your delicious Masoor Dal! Adjust the spice levels according to your taste.
