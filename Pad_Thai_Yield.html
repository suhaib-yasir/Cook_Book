Pad Thai Yield

Immerse yourself in the bustling street food scene of Thailand as you delve into the vibrant tapestry of Pad Thai. Picture the sizzle of a street-side wok, where rice noodles mingle with the intoxicating notes of tamarind, fish sauce, and lime. Each element harmonizes to create a culinary masterpiece that balances sweet, savory, and tangy flavors. Garnished with crunchy peanuts, fresh sprouts, and fragrant cilantro, Pad Thai is not just a dish; it's an invitation to experience the soul of Thai cuisine in every aromatic strand and flavorful bite.

Ingredients

  1. 8 oz. rice noodles, broken in half
  2. 6 tbsp. peanut or vegetable oil, divided
  3. 1 lb. medium shrimp, peeled, deveined, tails removed
  4. 3 large eggs
  5. 3 tbsp. palm sugar
  6. 3 tbsp. Thai fish sauce
  7. 2 tbsp. tamarind puree
  8. 1 tbsp. fresh lime juice, plus lime wedges for serving
  9. 1/4 tsp. cayenne pepper
  10. 1 medium shallot, finely chopped (about 3 tbsp.)
  11. 3 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  12. 6 scallions, cut into pieces
  13. 1 cup bean sprouts
  14. 1/4 cup coarsely chopped peanuts
  15. 2 tbsp coarsely chopped fresh cilantro (optional)

Directions

  1. If using dried noodles, soak noodles in boiling water until tender, 20 to 30 minutes.
  2. In a large wok over high heat, heat 1 tablespoon oil. Add shrimp and cook until just cooked through and pink, 2 to 3 minutes. Transfer to a bowl.
  3. In the same wok, heat 1 tablespoon oil. Whisk eggs and cook until just set, 1 to 2 minutes. Transfer to the bowl with shrimp.
  4. Whisk palm sugar, fish sauce, tamarind concentrate, lime juice, cayenne, 2 tablespoons oil, and 1 tablespoon water in a bowl until combined.
  5. In the same wok over medium-high heat, heat 2 tablespoons oil. Cook shallot and garlic until lightly golden, about 1 minute. Add scallions and cook until softened, 1 to 2 minutes. Stir in the sauce and bring to a simmer.
  6. Add eggs, shrimp, and noodles. Cook, tossing constantly, until warmed through and noodles are softened, about 2 minutes more. Add bean sprouts and peanuts, toss again to combine.
  7. Divide Pad Thai among plates. Top with cilantro (if using).

Enjoy this delicious and flavorful Pad Thai, a classic Thai street food dish! Adjust the ingredients and spice levels according to your taste preferences.
