Ladoo

Ladoo, the quintessential Indian sweet, stands as a symbol of joy and festivity, transcending regional boundaries with its myriad avatars. Whether it's the gram flour-infused Besan Ladoo or the coconut-laden Coconut Ladoo, these bite-sized orbs encapsulate the essence of simplicity and sweetness. Crafted from a harmonious blend of flour, ghee, and sugar, and often adorned with nuts or aromatic spices, ladoos carry the cultural weight of completeness and unity. Beyond being a delectable treat, ladoos have ingrained themselves as joyful companions during celebrations, festivals, and auspicious occasions, weaving sweetness into the fabric of Indian culinary traditions.

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  2. 1/2 cup water (approximately, for making the batter)
  3. A pinch of baking soda (optional)
  4. Oil or ghee (for frying)
  5. 1 cup sugar
  6. 1/2 cup water
  7. A pinch of cardamom powder
  8. A few saffron strands (optional)
  9. Chopped nuts for garnish (almonds, cashews, pistachios)

Directions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine besan and water to form a smooth, lump-free batter. The consistency should be similar to pancake batter. Add a pinch of baking soda if desired.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a deep frying pan.
  3. Hold a perforated ladle or slotted spoon over the hot oil and pour some batter onto it. Tap or swirl the ladle to let small droplets of batter fall into the hot oil, forming tiny boondis.
  4. Fry until the boondis are golden brown. Repeat until all the batter is used.
  5. Remove the fried boondis and place them on paper towels to absorb excess oil.
  6. In a separate pan, combine sugar and water to make a sugar syrup. Boil until it reaches a one-string consistency.
  7. Add cardamom powder and saffron strands to the syrup. Mix well.
  8. Add the fried boondis to the sugar syrup and mix well, ensuring that the boondis are well-coated with the syrup. Let it rest for a few minutes.
  9. Once the mixture is warm enough to handle, take small portions and shape them into round ladoos. You can use your hands or a small round mold for shaping.
  10. Garnish each ladoo with chopped nuts while they are still warm and pliable.
  11. Let the ladoos cool and set for a few hours.

Your delicious Boondi Ladoos are ready to be enjoyed! They make for a delightful festive or celebratory sweet. Adjust the sweetness and add your favorite nuts for a personal touch.
