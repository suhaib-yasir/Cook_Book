Sri Lankan Crab Curry

Prepare to embark on a tantalizing culinary journey with Sri Lankan Crab Curry, a dish that effortlessly marries the briny richness of crab with a symphony of aromatic spices. Bathed in a coconut milk-infused curry, the succulent crab meat absorbs the flavors of curry leaves, turmeric, and fenugreek, creating a harmonious blend of spicy and savory notes. This dish is a testament to Sri Lanka's culinary prowess, promising a taste of the ocean and the vibrant spices that define this coastal gem.

Ingredients

  1. 2 tbsp coconut oil
  2. 1/2 onion, roughly chopped
  3. 1 inch fresh ginger, minced
  4. 6 whole garlic cloves, roughly chopped
  5. 2 tbsp unroasted curry powder
  6. ½ tsp red chilli powder or a bit more to taste
  7. 1 tbsp tamarind paste
  8. 2 tsp sugar
  9. 3 fresh red or green chillies
  10. 8 curry leaves
  11. 2 pandan leaves
  12. 400 ml coconut milk
  13. Salt to taste
  14. For the Sri Lankan Curry Powder:
  15. 4 tsp coriander seeds
  16. 3 tsp cumin seeds
  17. 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  18. 2 tsp black peppercorns
  19. 1 tsp fennel seeds

Directions

  1. Finely grind all of the curry powder spices and set aside.
  2. Heat a large saucepan or clay pot over medium heat. When hot, add the rice and grated coconut and dry Fry until lightly browned and fragrant. Transfer to a pestle and mortar and pound to a fine powder. Set aside.
  3. Add about 2 tbsp coconut oil to a large pan or clay pot. You will need a large enough pot to be able To stir the crabs. Heat the oil over medium heat.
  4. Add the onion, ginger, and garlic and sauté for a few minutes until the onions have softened.
  5. Now add the curry powder, chili powder, and coconut/rice mix. Cook for 2-3 minutes until the spices Become fragrant.
  6. Add the tamarind paste, sugar, chili peppers, curry leaves, and pandan plus ¾ of the coconut milk. Then season with ¼ tsp salt, more can be added later to taste. Bring to a boil.
  7. Place the crabs in the sauce and gently stir to coat the crabs in the sauce.
  8. Bring this back to a boil on medium-high heat then lower the heat to medium. Cover and simmer for 15-20 minutes until the crabs are just cooked through.
  9. Make sure to turn the crabs halfway through to ensure they are well coated in the sauce.
  10. Add extra coconut milk if you prefer it creamier or up to ½ cup water to dilute it if you like.
  11. Serve warm with steamed rice or bread.

Enjoy the rich and aromatic flavors of Sri Lankan Crab Curry! Adjust the spice levels according to your preference.
