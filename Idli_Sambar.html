Idli Sambar

Embarking on a culinary journey to South India, the trio of Idli, Sambar, and Coconut Chutney emerges as a symphony of flavors, textures, and cultural richness. Idli, the feather-light steamed rice cakes, embodies simplicity and wholesomeness, acting as the perfect canvas for the dance of tastes that follows. Paired with Sambar, a robust and tangy lentil-based stew enriched with a medley of vegetables, the combination unfolds as a harmonious blend of spice and comfort.

Ingredients

  1. 2 cups idli rice
  2. 1 cup urad dal (black gram dal)
  3. Salt to taste
  4. Water (for soaking and grinding)
  5. 1/2 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas), cooked
  6. 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, beans, potatoes, drumsticks), chopped
  7. 1 onion, chopped
  8. 1 tomato, chopped
  9. 2 tablespoons sambar powder
  10. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  11. 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (optional)
  12. Tamarind pulp (extracted from a small lemon-sized tamarind)
  13. Salt to taste
  14. Water
  15. 2 tablespoons oil
  16. 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  17. 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  18. A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  19. Few curry leaves
  20. 1 cup fresh grated coconut
  21. 2 tablespoons roasted chana dal (dalia)
  22. 2-3 green chilies (adjust to taste)
  23. A small piece of ginger
  24. Salt to taste
  25. Water (as needed)
  26. 1 tablespoon oil
  27. 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  28. 1/2 teaspoon urad dal
  29. Few curry leaves

Directions

  1. Wash the idli rice and urad dal separately.
  2. Soak them in water for about 4-6 hours.
  3. Grind the urad dal to a smooth and fluffy consistency using as little water as possible.
  4. Grind the rice to a slightly coarse texture.
  5. Mix the urad dal batter and rice batter together. Add salt and mix well.
  6. Allow the batter to ferment overnight or for 8-10 hours.
  7. Grease the idli molds with oil.
  8. Pour the batter into the molds and steam them in an idli steamer for about 10-12 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean.
  9. Once done, remove the idlis from the molds using a spoon.
  10. Serve hot with sambar and coconut chutney.
  11. Pressure cook the toor dal until it becomes soft and mushy.
  12. In a pot, add chopped vegetables, onions, tomatoes, sambar powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, tamarind pulp, and salt.
  13. Add enough water to cover the vegetables and bring it to a boil. Cook until the vegetables are tender.
  14. Add the cooked toor dal to the boiling mixture. Stir well and let it simmer for a few minutes.
  15. In a separate pan, heat oil for tempering.
  16. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Let them splutter.
  17. Pour the tempering over the sambar and mix well.
  18. Adjust the consistency of the sambar by adding more water if needed.
  19. Serve hot sambar with idlis.
  20. In a blender, combine grated coconut, roasted chana dal, green chilies, ginger, and salt.
  21. Add water gradually and blend to a smooth chutney consistency.
  22. In a small pan, heat oil for tempering.
  23. Add mustard seeds, urad dal, and curry leaves. Let them splutter.
  24. Pour the tempering over the chutney and mix well.
  25. Serve the coconut chutney with idlis and sambar.

Enjoy your delicious South Indian breakfast of Idli with Sambar and Coconut Chutney! Adjust the spice levels in the sambar and chutney according to your taste preferences.
