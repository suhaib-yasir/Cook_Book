Aloo Gobi

Aloo Gobi, a culinary embrace of simplicity and depth, captures the essence of comfort in North Indian kitchens. This time-honored dish gracefully unites the humble potato (aloo) and the versatile cauliflower (gobi) in a harmonious dance of flavors and textures. As the cauliflower absorbs the aromatic spices, it transforms into tender morsels that seamlessly intertwine with the succulent potatoes.

Ingredients

  1. 1 medium-sized cauliflower, cut into florets
  2. 2 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and cubed
  3. 1 large onion, finely chopped
  4. 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  5. 2 green chilies, chopped (adjust to taste)
  6. 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  7. 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  8. 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  9. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  10. 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  11. 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  12. 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  13. Salt to taste
  14. Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  15. 2 tablespoons oil

Directions

  1. Cut the cauliflower into small florets.
  2. Peel and cube the potatoes.
  3. Boil water in a large pot and add a pinch of salt.
  4. Add cauliflower florets and cubed potatoes. Parboil for about 5 minutes.
  5. Drain the water and set aside the partially cooked vegetables.
  6. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds.
  7. Once they splutter, add chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until onions are golden brown.
  8. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  9. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil begins to separate.
  10. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.
  11. Add the partially cooked cauliflower and potatoes. Mix until the vegetables are coated with the masala.
  12. Lower the heat, cover the pan, and let it simmer. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  13. Cook until the vegetables are tender but not mushy. It usually takes about 15-20 minutes.
  14. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  15. Serve hot with chapati or rice.

Enjoy your delicious Aloo Gobi! Adjust the spice levels according to your taste preferences.
