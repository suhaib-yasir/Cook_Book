South Indian Prawn Curry

Allow your senses to be whisked away to the sun-kissed shores of South India with the enchanting South Indian Prawn Curry. As the aroma of curry leaves and mustard seeds fills the air, the curry unfolds into a mesmerizing tale of coastal indulgence. Succulent prawns, bathed in a luscious blend of coconut milk and an array of spices, create a symphony of flavors that dance on your palate. Slow-cooked to perfection, the curry captures the essence of South Indian culinary artistry, where each spoonful is a journey into a world where spice meets subtlety, leaving an indelible mark on your culinary wanderlust.

Ingredients

  1. 1/2 lb prawns (16-20 count)
  2. 1/2 tsp turmeric
  3. 1/2 tsp kashmiri chili powder
  4. Sprinkle of salt
  5. Enough oil to shallow fry the prawns
  6. 1 tsp garlic ginger paste
  7. 2 tsp Indian restaurant spice mix
  8. 1 tsp kashmiri chili powder
  9. 1/4 tsp black pepper
  10. 1/2 tsp kosher salt
  11. 3 Tbsp oil (for curry)
  12. 3 cardamom pods (green)
  13. 2-inch cinnamon stick (cassia bark)
  14. 1/2 tsp mustard seed
  15. 1/4 tsp cumin seed
  16. 12-15 fresh curry leaves
  17. 1 Tbsp garlic/ginger paste
  18. 2 green chilies
  19. 1 Tbsp tomato paste
  20. 15 oz curry base
  21. 1/2 cup coconut milk
  22. 2 tbsp diced tomato
  23. 1 green chili (diced)

Directions

  1. Gently toss prawns with turmeric, kashmiri chili powder, and salt.
  2. Preheat oil in a pan, add garlic ginger paste, and fry until sizzling.
  3. Cook prawns until bottom turns pink, then flip and cook until slightly opaque.
  4. Make the spice mix.
  5. Dilute tomato paste to the consistency of passata.
  6. Heat oil in a pan, add cardamom, cinnamon stick, mustard seed, cumin seed, and curry leaves.
  7. Add garlic/ginger paste and green chilies, cook until paste stops sputtering.
  8. Add spice mix, stir for 30 seconds.
  9. Turn up the heat, add diluted tomato paste, stir until bubbles form.
  10. Add curry base gradually, stirring until craters form.
  11. Turn heat to low, add coconut milk, stir. Add diced green chili, simmer for 3-4 minutes.
  12. Add diced tomatoes, stir. Add pre-cooked prawns and cook until warmed through.
  13. Serve with rice and/or parathas or chapatis.

Enjoy the rich and aromatic flavors of South Indian Prawn Curry! Adjust the spice levels according to your preference.
