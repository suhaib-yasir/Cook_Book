Samosa

Samosa, a delectable pyramid of warmth and flavor, holds a special place in the heart of Indian street food. This golden, triangular pastry encases a tantalizing mixture of spiced potatoes, peas, and sometimes meat, creating a harmonious blend of textures. Deep-fried to perfection, the crispy exterior gives way to a fragrant, seasoned filling, making each bite a culinary journey. Often served with tangy tamarind chutney or mint sauce, samosas are not just a snack; they're an embodiment of the diverse and irresistible flavors that define Indian cuisine. Whether enjoyed as a quick bite on the go or a delightful appetizer at gatherings, samosas are a culinary delight that transcends boundaries and brings people together in the shared joy of good food.

Ingredients

  1. 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  2. 1/4 cup ghee or oil
  3. 1/2 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  4. A pinch of salt
  5. Water (as needed)
  6. 3 large potatoes, boiled, peeled, and mashed
  7. 1 cup green peas, boiled
  8. 1 tablespoon oil
  9. 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  10. 1 tablespoon ginger, finely chopped
  11. 1 tablespoon green chili, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  12. 1/2 cup onions, finely chopped
  13. 1/2 cup carrots, finely chopped
  14. 1/2 cup green beans, finely chopped
  15. 1/2 cup cauliflower, finely chopped
  16. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  17. 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  18. 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  19. 1 teaspoon garam masala
  20. Salt to taste
  21. Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  22. Oil for deep frying

Directions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, carom seeds, salt, and ghee (or oil).
  2. Mix well until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  3. Gradually add water and knead to form a firm and smooth dough.
  4. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.
  5. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  6. Add chopped ginger, green chili, and onions. Saut√© until the onions become translucent.
  7. Add chopped carrots, green beans, cauliflower, and boiled peas. Cook until the vegetables are tender.
  8. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.
  9. Add the mashed potatoes and mix thoroughly, ensuring the spices coat the vegetables evenly.
  10. Stir in chopped fresh coriander leaves. Remove from heat and let the filling cool.
  11. Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each portion into a ball.
  12. Roll out each ball into a thin oval or round shape.
  13. Cut each rolled-out circle in half to form two semi-circles.
  14. Create a cone shape by folding each semi-circle and sealing the edges with a little water.
  15. Fill the cone with the prepared potato filling and seal the open edge, pinching it tightly to ensure it's closed.
  16. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying.
  17. Gently slide the filled samosas into the hot oil.
  18. Fry on medium heat until they turn golden brown and crisp.
  19. Remove the fried samosas and place them on paper towels to absorb excess oil.
  20. Serve hot samosas with mint chutney or tamarind sauce.

Enjoy your homemade Samosas! They are perfect as a snack or appetizer and are sure to be a hit at any gathering. Adjust the spice levels according to your taste preferences.
