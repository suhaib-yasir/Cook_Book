Gajar Ka Halwa

Gajar ka Halwa, a winter delicacy with roots in familial kitchens, is a testament to the culinary magic that can be woven from humble carrots. Slow-cooked in ghee, milk, and sugar, the grated carrots undergo a metamorphosis into a rich, aromatic delight. Garnished with an array of nuts, Gajar ka Halwa is not just a dessert; it's a sensory journey that captures the essence of warmth, festivity, and the love that goes into crafting each spoonful.  

    

Ingredients

  1. 1 kg carrots, grated
  2. 1 liter full-fat milk
  3. 1 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  4. 1/2 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  5. 1/2 cup chopped mixed nuts (almonds, cashews, pistachios)
  6. 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  7. A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  8. Raisins for garnish

Directions

  1. Wash, peel, and grate the carrots.
  2. In a heavy-bottomed pan, heat ghee and add the grated carrots. Sauté on medium heat until the carrots are cooked and the raw smell disappears.
  3. Meanwhile, in another pan, heat the milk and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat and let it simmer until it reduces to half, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add the reduced milk to the cooked carrots and mix well. Cook on low heat until the mixture thickens.
  5. Add sugar and continue to cook, stirring frequently, until the halwa reaches a thick consistency and the ghee starts to separate.
  6. Add cardamom powder, saffron strands (if using), and chopped nuts. Mix well and cook for an additional few minutes.
  7. Remove from heat and garnish with raisins.
  8. Serve warm, and enjoy the delicious Gajar ka Halwa!

Enjoy the rich and delicious Gajar ka Halwa! Adjust the sugar and ghee quantities according to your taste preference.
