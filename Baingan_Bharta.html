Baingan Bharta

Embark on a culinary journey with Baingan Bharta, a North Indian delight that transforms the ordinary into the extraordinary. This smoky revelation unfolds as a tale of roasted and mashed eggplant (baingan), where the charred aroma imparts a distinctive character. A medley of aromatic spices dances harmoniously, enhancing the natural richness of this humble vegetable. Tomatoes lend a subtle tanginess, creating a symphony of flavors that evoke warmth and comfort. Baingan Bharta is not just a dish; it’s a sensory experience, an ode to the artistry embedded in the heart of Indian cuisine, inviting you to savor the soulful essence of tradition on your plate

Ingredients

  1. 1 large eggplant (baingan)
  2. 2 tablespoons oil
  3. 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  4. 1 large onion, finely chopped
  5. 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  6. 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  7. 1 green chili, chopped (adjust to taste)
  8. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  9. 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  10. 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  11. Salt to taste
  12. Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Directions

  1. Roast the eggplant directly over an open flame or on a gas stove. Turn it occasionally until the skin is charred, and the flesh becomes soft. Alternatively, you can also roast it in an oven.
  2. Let the roasted eggplant cool. Once cooled, peel off the charred skin. Mash the flesh well and set aside.
  3. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  5. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes and green chili. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil starts to separate.
  7. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  8. Add the mashed eggplant to the masala mixture. Mix well, ensuring the eggplant is coated with the spices.
  9. Simmer the bharta for 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  11. Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.

Enjoy your homemade Baingan Bharta! Adjust the spice levels according to your taste preferences. The smoky flavor from the roasted eggplant adds a unique touch to this dish.
