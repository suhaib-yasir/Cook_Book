Rice Kheer

Kheer, the timeless rice pudding, invites you to savor the essence of comfort and nostalgia. Simmered patiently to achieve the perfect consistency, the amalgamation of rice, milk, and sugar creates a creamy canvas for the dance of cardamom and the crunch of assorted nuts. Kheer, with its warm embrace, is not just a dessert but a vessel for cherished memories and familial bonds.  

    

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup Basmati rice
  2. 4 cups whole milk
  3. 1 cup sugar
  4. 1/2 cup condensed milk
  5. 1/2 cup chopped nuts (almonds, cashews, pistachios)
  6. 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  7. 1 tablespoon ghee (clarified butter)
  8. A pinch of saffron strands (optional)

Directions

  1. Wash the rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes.
  2. Heat ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan and add the soaked rice. Sauté for a few minutes until the rice turns light golden brown.
  3. Add the whole milk to the rice and bring it to a boil. Then, reduce the heat to low and let it simmer until the rice is fully cooked and the milk thickens.
  4. Add sugar, condensed milk, and chopped nuts. Stir well and continue to cook until the kheer reaches the desired consistency.
  5. Add cardamom powder and saffron strands (if using), and mix well.
  6. Remove from heat and let it cool. The kheer will thicken further as it cools.
  7. Serve chilled, garnished with additional nuts if desired.

Enjoy the rich and creamy goodness of homemade Kheer! Adjust the sugar and nuts according to your taste.
