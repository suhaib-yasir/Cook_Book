Palak Paneer

In the kaleidoscope of Indian cuisine, Palak Paneer emerges as a beacon of wholesome warmth and culinary finesse. This iconic vegetarian dish beckons with its vibrant green hues, embodying the fusion of nutrition and indulgence. A symphony of flavors unfolds as succulent cubes of paneer find themselves enveloped in a velvety puree of fresh spinach (palak), meticulously seasoned with an orchestra of aromatic spices.

Ingredients

  1. 3 cups fresh spinach leaves, washed and blanched
  2. 1 cup fenugreek leaves (optional), washed and blanched
  3. 2 green chilies
  4. 1-inch piece of ginger
  5. 4-5 cloves of garlic
  6. 200g paneer (cubed)
  7. 1 cup onions, finely chopped
  8. 1 cup tomatoes, finely chopped
  9. 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  10. 1 teaspoon garam masala
  11. 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  12. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  13. 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  14. Salt to taste
  15. 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  16. 2 tablespoons cream (optional)
  17. Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Directions

  1. Blanch the spinach and fenugreek leaves in hot water for 2-3 minutes. Drain and transfer them to ice-cold water to retain the green color.
  2. Blend the blanched spinach and fenugreek leaves, green chilies, ginger, and garlic into a smooth paste. Set aside.
  3. Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add chopped onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and the oil starts to separate.
  6. Add garam masala, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Pour in the prepared palak paste. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the raw smell disappears and the mixture thickens.
  8. Add the cubed paneer to the spinach mixture. Stir gently to coat the paneer with the masala.
  9. Simmer the curry for another 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend.
  10. If using cream, add it at this stage and mix well. This adds richness to the dish.
  11. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  12. Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.

Enjoy your homemade Palak Paneer! Adjust the spice levels and consistency according to your liking.
