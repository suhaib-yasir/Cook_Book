Air Fryer Thai Coconut Shrimp

Embark on a tropical journey with our Air Fryer Thai Coconut Shrimp - a dish that encapsulates the essence of Southeast Asian cuisine. Plump shrimp, coated in a luscious blend of Thai-inspired coconut goodness, are air-fried to achieve the perfect balance of crispy exteriors and succulent interiors. Accompanied by an exotic coconut-infused dipping sauce, this dish is an invitation to savor the harmonious interplay of flavors - the sweetness of coconut, the warmth of Thai spices, and the irresistible crunch of perfectly air-fried shrimp. It's not just a meal; it's an expedition into the heart of culinary bliss.

Ingredients

  1. 1 pound medium shrimp, peeled and deveined
  2. 1/2 cup whole-wheat flour (can use coconut flour to make dish gluten-free)
  3. 2 eggs, beaten
  4. 1/2 cup unsweetened shredded coconut
  5. 1/2 cup panko (can use plain or whole-wheat or gluten-free)
  6. 1/2 teaspoon salt
  7. 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  8. Juice from ½ large lime
  9. 1/2 tablespoon ginger, grated
  10. 1-2 tablespoons fish sauce; or soy sauce
  11. 1/2 jalapeno, finely diced
  12. 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  13. Chili sauce or small red chilies

Directions

  1. Pat shrimp dry with a paper towel; set shrimp aside.
  2. Put flour into the first small mixing bowl, beat eggs in the second small bowl, and mix together coconut, panko, salt, and pepper in the third small bowl.
  3. Dredge shrimp through the flour, then through the beaten eggs, and then through the coconut mixture, coating well each time. Set shrimp to the side after coating.
  4. Preheat Air Fryer at 400°F for five minutes. Lightly spray Air Fryer basket with nonstick spray.
  5. Place half the shrimp in the basket and cook at 400°F until golden, about 4 minutes. Flip shrimp and spray the basket again with nonstick spray. Cook shrimp for another 4 minutes and, if needed, another 2-3 minutes beyond that. Repeat with remaining shrimp.
  6. Squeeze lime juice from the half lime into a medium mixing bowl. Add ginger, fish sauce (or soy sauce), jalapeno, brown sugar, and red chili sauce (or chilies); mix until well combined. Serve as a dipping sauce for shrimp.

Enjoy the delightful flavors of Air Fryer Thai Coconut Shrimp with a tangy and sweet dipping sauce! Adjust the spice levels and ingredients according to your taste.
