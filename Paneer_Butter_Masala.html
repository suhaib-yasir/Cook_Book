Paneer Butter Masala

Paneer Butter Masala, a culinary jewel that radiates warmth and indulgence, beckons connoisseurs of Indian cuisine into a realm of sensory delight. This iconic dish, hailing from the heart of North India, envelops the senses in a comforting embrace of rich flavors and creamy textures. Imagine tender cubes of paneer, bathed in a luscious tomato gravy that whispers tales of spice-laden journeys through aromatic kitchens.

Ingredients

  1. 250g paneer (cubed)
  2. 1/2 cup yogurt
  3. 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  4. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  5. Salt to taste
  6. 2 tablespoons butter
  7. 1 tablespoon oil
  8. 1 cup onion (finely chopped)
  9. 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  10. 1 cup tomato puree
  11. 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  12. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  13. 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  14. 1 teaspoon garam masala
  15. Salt to taste
  16. 1 cup milk or cream
  17. 1 tablespoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  18. Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Directions

  1. In a bowl, mix yogurt, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt.
  2. Add paneer cubes to the marinade, ensuring they are well coated.
  3. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Heat a pan and add some oil.
  5. Fry the marinated paneer cubes until they turn golden brown. Set aside.
  6. In the same pan, add butter and a bit of oil.
  7. Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  8. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1-2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  9. Add tomato puree and cook until the oil starts to separate from the masala.
  10. Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook the spices for a couple of minutes.
  11. Allow the mixture to cool slightly and then blend it into a smooth paste using a blender or hand blender.
  12. Pour the blended mixture back into the pan.
  13. Add milk or cream and mix well. Adjust the consistency by adding water if needed.
  14. Crush kasuri methi between your palms and add it to the gravy.
  15. Gently add the fried paneer cubes to the gravy and simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  16. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  17. Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.

Enjoy your homemade Paneer Butter Masala! Adjust the spice levels and consistency according to your preference.
