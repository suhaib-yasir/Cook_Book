Mandi

Mandi, a culinary treasure originating from the Arabian Peninsula, is a feast for both the eyes and the palate. This traditional rice dish is prepared with tender meat, often lamb or chicken, slow-cooked to perfection with a medley of aromatic spices. The rice, soaked in the essence of the meat, becomes a canvas for the symphony of flavors. The beauty of Mandi lies in its simplicity – a celebration of quality ingredients and slow-cooking techniques. Served with a side of flavorful broth, Mandi offers a journey through the rich tapestry of Arabian cuisine, making it a culinary adventure that lingers in the memory long after the last bite.  

    

Ingredients

  1. Chicken or lamb, cut into pieces
  2. Basmati rice
  3. Onions, thinly sliced
  4. Tomatoes, chopped
  5. Carrots, sliced
  6. Potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  7. Garlic cloves, minced
  8. Ginger, grated
  9. Green chili peppers, chopped
  10. Ground black lime (loomi)
  11. Mandi spice mix (a blend of cumin, coriander, cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, bay leaves, black lime, and black pepper)
  12. Ghee or vegetable oil
  13. Chicken or vegetable broth
  14. Salt to taste
  15. Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Directions

  1. Rinse the basmati rice in cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. In a large pot, heat ghee or vegetable oil. Add sliced onions and cook until golden brown.
  3. Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and chopped green chili peppers. Sauté until fragrant.
  4. Introduce the chicken or lamb pieces and brown them on all sides.
  5. Stir in chopped tomatoes, carrots, potatoes, and the Mandi spice mix. Cook for a few minutes until the vegetables are slightly softened.
  6. Add the soaked and drained basmati rice to the pot. Mix well to coat the rice with the spice mixture.
  7. Pour in enough chicken or vegetable broth to cover the rice and meat. Season with salt to taste.
  8. Add ground black lime (loomi) for a distinctive flavor.
  9. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and simmer on low heat until the rice is cooked, and the meat is tender.
  10. Once done, fluff the rice gently with a fork and mix in the meat and vegetables.
  11. Garnish with chopped fresh coriander leaves.
  12. Serve hot, and enjoy your flavorful Mandi!

Enjoy your flavorful and aromatic Chicken Mandi! Adjust the spice levels to suit your taste preferences.
