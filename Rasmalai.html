Rasmalai

Rasmalai, a dessert fit for royalty, transforms the humble paneer into a velvety indulgence. Nestled in a pool of saffron-kissed milk, these flattened spheres are adorned with a crown of cardamom, pistachios, and almonds. The interplay of flavors and the silkiness of the milk infusion make Rasmalai not just a sweet treat but a symphony of decadence, elevating dessert to an art form.

Ingredients

  1. 1 liter milk
  2. 1/4 cup vinegar or lemon juice
  3. 1/2 cup sugar
  4. Cardamom powder
  5. Chopped nuts for garnish

Directions

  1. Boil milk and add vinegar/lemon juice to curdle it. Strain the chenna (curdled milk).
  2. Knead the chenna into a smooth dough and shape into flat discs.
  3. Boil sugar and water to make a syrup. Add cardamom powder.
  4. Add the chenna discs to the syrup and simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  5. Garnish with chopped nuts and refrigerate before serving.

Enjoy the delightful and creamy Rasmalai! Adjust the sweetness and the thickness of the rabri according to your taste.
