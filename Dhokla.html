Dhokla

Dhokla, a steamed spongy cake originating from the western Indian state of Gujarat, is a culinary gem celebrated for its lightness and unique texture. Crafted from fermented rice and chickpea flour, this delicacy undergoes a magical transformation during the steaming process, resulting in a fluffy, airy creation. Infused with a tempering of mustard seeds, curry leaves, and green chilies, Dhokla offers a harmonious blend of flavors. Served with a side of tangy tamarind or mint chutney, Dhokla stands as a testament to the diversity of Indian snacks, captivating palates with its delightful combination of softness and zesty freshness. Perfect for breakfast, tea-time, or as an appetizer, Dhokla exemplifies the art of creating culinary magic through simplicity.

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  2. 1/2 cup semolina (sooji/rava)
  3. 1 cup yogurt
  4. 1/2 teaspoon ginger paste
  5. 1/2 teaspoon green chili paste (adjust to taste)
  6. Salt to taste
  7. 1 teaspoon eno fruit salt (or 1/2 teaspoon baking soda)
  8. 2 tablespoons oil
  9. 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  10. 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  11. 1/2 teaspoon sesame seeds (optional)
  12. 8-10 curry leaves
  13. 2 green chilies, slit
  14. A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  15. 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves for garnish
  16. 1 cup fresh coriander leaves
  17. 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves
  18. 2-3 green chilies
  19. 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  20. Salt to taste
  21. Water (as needed to blend)

Directions

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine gram flour, semolina, yogurt, ginger paste, green chili paste, and salt.
  2. Add water gradually to make a smooth batter without any lumps. The batter should be of pouring consistency.
  3. Allow the batter to rest for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Just before steaming, add eno fruit salt to the batter and mix well. You'll notice the batter becoming frothy.
  5. Grease a dhokla plate or a shallow dish.
  6. Pour the batter into the greased plate and place it in a steamer.
  7. Steam the batter for 15-20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  8. Heat oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, sesame seeds (if using), curry leaves, green chilies, and asafoetida. Let the mustard seeds splutter.
  9. Pour this tempering over the steamed dhokla.
  10. Allow the dhokla to cool for a few minutes, then cut it into squares or diamonds.
  11. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  12. Blend coriander leaves, mint leaves, green chilies, lemon juice, and salt to make a smooth chutney. Add water as needed.
  13. Serve the dhokla with green chutney or any chutney of your choice.

Enjoy your homemade Dhokla! It's a light and fluffy snack that is perfect for breakfast or as a tea-time treat. Adjust the spice levels and toppings according to your taste.
