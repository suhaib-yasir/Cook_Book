Gulab Jamun

Picture, if you will, the allure of Gulab Jamun, those enchanting spheres of bliss that beckon with their golden sheen. Crafted from a velvety blend of khoya and flour, these dumplings transcend mere sweetness. Deep-fried to a golden perfection and immersed in a fragrant rose and cardamom-infused sugar syrup, each Gulab Jamun is a testament to the mastery of Indian sweet craftsmanship, offering a harmonious dance of textures and flavors.

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup khoya (milk solids)
  2. 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  3. 1/4 cup paneer (optional)
  4. 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  5. Ghee for frying
  6. For Sugar Syrup:
  7. 1 cup sugar
  8. 1 cup water
  9. Cardamom pods (optional)

Directions

  1. Boil milk and add vinegar/lemon juice to curdle it. Strain the chenna (curdled milk).
  2. Knead the chenna
  3. Mix khoya, flour, paneer, and baking soda to form a smooth dough.
  4. Shape the dough into small balls.
  5. Heat ghee in a pan and fry the balls until golden brown.
  6. Prepare sugar syrup by boiling sugar and water. Add cardamom pods if desired.
  7. Soak the fried balls in the sugar syrup for a few hours before serving.

Enjoy the sweet and delightful Gulab Jamuns! Adjust the size of the balls and the sweetness of the sugar syrup according to your preference.
