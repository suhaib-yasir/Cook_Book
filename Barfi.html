Barfi

Barfi, a confectionary masterpiece in Indian sweets, is a delectable treat that captivates the senses with its irresistible sweetness and rich textures. Crafted from condensed milk, sugar, and ghee, Barfi is a celebration of simplicity and sophistication. The name “Barfi” itself is derived from the Persian word “barf,” meaning snow, alluding to the dessert's melt-in-the-mouth consistency.

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup pistachios, shelled and ground into a fine powder
  2. 1 cup khoya (mawa), crumbled
  3. 1 cup sugar
  4. 1/2 cup water
  5. A pinch of cardamom powder
  6. Ghee (clarified butter) for greasing the tray
  7. Chopped pistachios for garnish

Directions

  1. Remove the shells from the pistachios and grind them into a fine powder using a food processor or grinder.
  2. If using store-bought khoya, crumble it. If making khoya at home, cook milk until it thickens and solidifies to form khoya.
  3. In a pan, heat crumbled khoya on low flame. Add the ground pistachio powder and mix well.
  4. In a separate pan, combine sugar and water to make a sugar syrup. Boil until it reaches a one-string consistency.
  5. Add the sugar syrup to the khoya-pistachio mixture and stir continuously to avoid lumps.
  6. Cook the mixture on low to medium flame until it thickens and starts leaving the sides of the pan. Add cardamom powder and mix well.
  7. Grease a tray or plate with ghee.
  8. Transfer the thickened mixture onto the greased tray and spread it evenly with a spatula.
  9. Garnish the surface with chopped pistachios, gently pressing them into the mixture.
  10. Allow the barfi to cool and set at room temperature for a few hours or refrigerate for quicker setting.
  11. Once set, cut the barfi into square or diamond-shaped pieces using a sharp knife.

Your delicious Pista Barfi is ready to be served! Enjoy this festive and nutty sweet treat. Adjust the sugar quantity based on your preference for sweetness.
