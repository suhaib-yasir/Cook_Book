Mysore Pak

Mysore Pak, an epitome of South Indian sweet indulgence, is a confectionery masterpiece that traces its roots to the royal city of Mysuru. This delectable treat is crafted from a harmonious trinity of besan (chickpea flour), ghee, and sugar, meticulously blended to achieve a sublime texture. Its golden hue and melt-in-the-mouth consistency are a testament to the culinary finesse embedded in South Indian traditions. As the sweet fragrance of ghee permeates, each bite unfolds a symphony of flavors â€” the nuttiness of besan, the richness of ghee, and the perfect sweetness. Mysore Pak transcends the ordinary, offering a culinary experience that resonates with the heritage and regality of its birthplace.

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  2. 1 cup ghee
  3. 1 cup sugar
  4. 1/4 cup water
  5. A pinch of cardamom powder
  6. Chopped nuts (optional, for garnish)

Directions

  1. Sieve the besan to remove any lumps. This step is important to get a smooth texture in the Mysore Pak.
  2. In a heavy-bottomed pan, heat ghee over low-medium heat.
  3. Add the sieved besan to the melted ghee and mix well.
  4. Cook the besan and ghee mixture over low-medium heat. Stir continuously to avoid lumps and ensure even cooking.
  5. As the mixture cooks, it will start to thicken. Keep stirring to avoid burning. The besan will release a nutty aroma, and the color will change to a light golden brown.
  6. At this point, the mixture will start leaving the sides of the pan. It should be smooth, without any visible grains of besan.
  7. In a separate pan, combine sugar and water. Bring it to a boil, stirring to dissolve the sugar.
  8. Boil the sugar syrup until it reaches a one-string consistency. To test, take a small amount of syrup between your thumb and forefinger. When you separate your fingers, a single thread or string should form.
  9. Once the sugar syrup is ready, gradually add it to the cooked besan mixture. Keep stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  10. Continue cooking the combined mixture over low heat. The mixture will further thicken.
  11. Add a pinch of cardamom powder for flavor. Mix well.
  12. Grease a tray or plate with a little ghee.
  13. Pour the thickened Mysore Pak mixture onto the greased tray.
  14. Spread the mixture evenly with a spatula. Optionally, garnish with chopped nuts like cashews or almonds.
  15. Allow the Mysore Pak to cool for a few minutes but cut it into pieces while it's still warm. This makes it easier to get clean cuts.
  16. Allow the Mysore Pak to cool completely and set at room temperature.

Your delicious Mysore Pak is ready to be enjoyed! It's a delightful sweet with a unique texture and rich flavor. Adjust the quantity of ghee and sugar based on your taste preferences.
