Shahi Tukda

Shahi Tukda, a jewel in the crown of Mughlai desserts, exudes grandeur with every golden bite. Slices of bread, crisped to perfection, are bathed in a saffron-kissed sugar syrup, imparting a heavenly sweetness. Crowned with a velvety layer of rabri, enriched with the essence of condensed milk, Shahi Tukda transcends the realm of ordinary sweets. Adorned with slivers of pistachios and almonds, each serving is a testament to culinary artistry, offering a royal indulgence that captivates both the eyes and the palate.

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup sugar
  2. 1/2 cup water
  3. A few saffron strands
  4. 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  5. 1 liter full-fat milk
  6. 1/2 cup sugar
  7. A pinch of saffron strands
  8. 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  9. 4-6 slices of bread (white or brown)
  10. Ghee or oil for frying
  11. Chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios) for garnish
  12. Edible silver foil (optional)

Directions

  1. In a pan, combine sugar and water for the sugar syrup. Bring it to a boil, stirring to dissolve the sugar.
  2. Add saffron strands and cardamom powder to the boiling sugar syrup. Simmer for a few minutes until it reaches a slightly sticky consistency.
  3. Set aside the sugar syrup to cool.
  4. In a wide, heavy-bottomed pan, bring the milk to a boil.
  5. Reduce the heat to low and simmer the milk, stirring frequently to avoid sticking to the bottom.
  6. Once the milk has reduced to half its original quantity, add sugar and saffron strands. Continue simmering.
  7. Keep stirring the milk until it thickens and attains a rabri-like consistency. This may take some time, but the result is a rich, condensed milk texture.
  8. Add cardamom powder and mix well.
  9. Once the rabri is thick and creamy, remove it from the heat and let it cool.
  10. Cut the bread slices into desired shapes (triangles or squares).
  11. Heat ghee or oil in a pan and fry the bread slices until golden brown on both sides. Drain excess oil on paper towels.
  12. Dip the fried bread slices into the prepared sugar syrup for a few seconds, ensuring they are coated on both sides.
  13. Arrange the sugar syrup-soaked bread slices in a serving dish.
  14. Pour the cooled rabri over the arranged bread slices.
  15. Garnish with chopped nuts and, if desired, edible silver foil for a royal touch.
  16. Serve Shahi Tukda chilled or at room temperature.

Enjoy your decadent Shahi Tukda! It's a classic Mughlai dessert with a perfect balance of textures and flavors. Adjust the sugar quantity according to your taste preferences.
